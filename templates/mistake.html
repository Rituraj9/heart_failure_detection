You Sit Too Much

You may have heard that "sitting is the new smoking." It's true: Spending a lot of time seated is bad for you. Couch potatoes are nearly twice as likely to get heart disease as those who are more active.Lack of exercise can harm your heart in many ways. For example, it can lead you to high blood pressure and unhealthy cholesterol levels.

3.Discomfort in one or both arms, or in the back, shoulders, neck, or jaw.
4.Unusual tiredness

You’re Under Strain

This includes both physical demand from extra pounds and the mental pressure from being stressed out too often. Over time, both can take a toll on your body.
Grow your stress management skills. You might meditate, work out, spend time with loved ones, get out in nature, or do anything else that’s healthy and that you enjoy.

You Smoke

Smoking isn’t just bad for your lungs. It’s one of the top enemies of your heart.If you go through a pack of cigarettes a day, you are more than twice as likely to have a heart attack as a person who has never smoked.
Do everything you can to kick the habit. No matter how long you’ve smoked, quitting can help get your heart and blood vessels back on track, and it can cut your risk of heart disease and heart attacks.
